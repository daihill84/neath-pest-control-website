Why Rats Love Neath's Drainage Systems

Neath and the surrounding valleys have a mixture of Victorian clay pipes and modern PVC systems. Over time, ground movement and wear lead to cracks and displaced joints. For a rat, a 20mm gap is an open door. Once they exit the pipe, they follow the warm air currents behind your wall cavities or up through the "dry" areas of your foundations, ending up in your loft.

The Failure of Traditional Baiting

Traditional pest control relies on poison. While this kills the resident rats, it leaves the "front door" open. New rats from the main sewer line will detect the vacant territory and move in within weeks. This is why residents in Resolven and Skewen often report "seasonal" rat problems. A CCTV drain survey is the only way to stop the cycle by identifying the structural fault.

What My High-Definition Survey Identifies

As an Ex-Pestokil specialist, I use industry-leading push-rod cameras to inspect every inch of your internal and external lines. We look for:

  • Redundant Junctions: Old pipes left behind after extensions or bathroom refits that provide perfect nesting spots.
  • Displaced Joints: Gaps between pipe sections caused by tree roots or ground subsidence.
  • Burrow Holes: Evidence of rats tunneling through the soil surrounding your pipes.
  • Incorrect Venting: Stack pipes that allow rats to climb directly into your roof space.

Installing Stainless Steel Rat Blockers

Once the entry point is confirmed, we don't need to dig up your garden. We use No-Dig Technology. I install professional-grade, dual-flap stainless steel rat blockers (Non-Return Valves). These are specifically designed for the UK drainage system.

Unlike plastic alternatives which rats can chew through in minutes, these high-grade steel units are impenetrable. They allow waste and water to flow freely out of your house, but the heavy-duty flaps remain locked against any upstream rodent movement. This is a permanent, poison-free solution.
